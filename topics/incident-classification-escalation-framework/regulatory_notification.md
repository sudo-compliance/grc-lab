# Regulatory Notification Requirements

This document defines the regulatory notification triggers and timelines applicable to UK financial services organisations operating under DORA, FCA, and PRA requirements.

---

## DORA — ICT-Related Incident Reporting (Articles 17–19)

### What Must Be Reported

Under DORA Article 18, a "major ICT-related incident" must be reported if it meets or exceeds thresholds across any of the following criteria:

- Number of clients or financial counterparts affected
- Duration of the incident
- Geographical spread
- Data losses (availability, authenticity, integrity, confidentiality)
- Criticality of the services affected
- Economic impact

### Reporting Timeline

| Report | Deadline | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Initial notification | Within 4 hours of classifying the incident as major, and no later than 24 hours from detection | Nature of the incident, initial classification, affected services, initial impact assessment |
| Intermediate report | Within 72 hours of the initial notification (or upon significant change in status) | Updated impact assessment, root cause analysis (if available), containment status, recovery actions |
| Final report | Within 1 month of resolution | Complete root cause analysis, total impact, remediation actions taken, lessons learned |

### Who Receives the Report

Reports are submitted to the relevant competent authority. For UK-authorised firms operating within the EU regulatory perimeter, this is coordinated through the relevant National Competent Authority. For UK-only firms, DORA obligations may apply indirectly through contractual obligations with EU-regulated counterparts.

---

## FCA — Material Incident Notification (SUP 15.3)

### What Must Be Reported

The FCA requires notification of any matter that could reasonably be expected to have a significant adverse impact on the firm's operations, reputation, or customers. In practice, this includes:

- Significant operational disruptions affecting customer access to services
- Cyber incidents involving confirmed or suspected data compromise affecting customers
- Material fraud events
- Incidents requiring activation of business continuity arrangements

### Reporting Timeline

The FCA does not prescribe a fixed-hour deadline but expects notification "immediately" when the firm becomes aware of a material incident. In practice, firms should target initial notification within the same timescales as DORA (within 4 hours of classification) where the incident is clearly material.

### How to Notify

Contact the FCA via the firm's usual supervisory contact or through the FCA Connect portal. Out-of-hours contact arrangements should be pre-established and documented.

---

## PRA — Operational Resilience (SS1/21, PS21/3)

The PRA expects firms to be able to remain within impact tolerances for important business services during severe but plausible disruption scenarios. Incidents that cause a firm to breach its defined impact tolerances for an important business service should be notified to the PRA promptly.

---

## Internal Decision Framework

Use the following to determine whether regulatory notification is required:

1. **Is regulated customer data confirmed or suspected to be compromised?** If yes, notification is likely required under both DORA and FCA SUP 15.3.

2. **Is a core customer-facing service unavailable or materially degraded beyond the defined impact tolerance?** If yes, notification is likely required under PRA SS1/21 and FCA SUP 15.3.

3. **Does the incident meet DORA Article 18 thresholds for a major ICT-related incident?** If yes, DORA reporting timelines apply.

4. **Is the incident being reported by media or receiving social media attention?** If yes, proactive notification to the FCA is strongly recommended even if formal thresholds are not clearly met.

If the answer to any of the above is "yes" or "uncertain," engage regulatory affairs immediately and err toward notification. Regulators consistently prefer to be informed early of incidents that may not ultimately require formal reporting, rather than to learn of material incidents late.

---

## Notification Register

All regulatory notifications (made or considered and determined not required) are recorded in the incident register with the decision rationale, the individual who made the determination, and the timestamp.
